# Myth and Objective-Function Cross-Engineering Audit

Status: `[CANONICAL]` `[Layer A/B bridge]`\
Established: 2026-07-17\
Applies to: safety, institutions, finance, information systems, human factors, GUI, KPI design, and AI-agent operations

> Language and authority boundary: this en-US document is an external audit GUI. The
> [Japanese source](myth-purpose-cross-engineering-audit.ja.md) remains canonical. Keep its mythic and religious
> vocabulary as functional architecture; do not turn this GUI into permission to execute a system.

## 1. Upper rule

Myth, philosophy, romance, religious background, and metaphor are not interchangeable flavor attached to
engineering. Determine, where possible, which objective, value, prohibition, stop condition, or future they retain.

Engineering implementation and mythic purpose are not identical, but neither has permission to erase the other.

- Myth and philosophy are the meaning-side wheel: what must be protected, gained, or never done.
- Engineering, resources, verification, institutions, budgets, and UI are the execution-side wheel that maps that
  purpose into reality without breaking it.

If either wheel is missing, do not call implementation success, regulatory compliance, profit, or KPI completion a
complete success.

## 2. “It is just flavor” alarm

Raise an objective-attenuation alarm when a process:

- deletes myth or metaphor as decoration;
- declares religious or philosophical background irrelevant to implementation without examining it;
- excludes values that do not fit a KPI;
- measures a GUI only by efficiency and removes user sovereignty, ritual, meaning, or exit;
- keeps capital recovery while deleting research purpose or gift-economy structure;
- deletes the purpose an institution was meant to protect in order to comply with the institution;
- drops stop conditions, exceptions, prohibitions, or narrative responsibility during compression.

Not every metaphor must appear directly in code, a contract, or a ledger. When it does not map directly, retain the
objective carried by the metaphor in a separate field.

### 2.1 Do not promote commercial KPI into a truth ruler

Market size, revenue, sponsor loss, enterprise adoption, staff count, procurement practice, and legal-incident counts
may all be important observations in their own environments. Ease of monetization, number of adopters, and size of
loss do not prove universal correctness for another product, user, gift economy, minority, or experiment.

Commercial and noncommercial or performer-sovereign systems may place different KPI on the same failure.

| Observed failure | KPI that may dominate in a commercial system | KPI that may dominate in a performer/experimental system |
|---|---|---|
| source endpoint changes | wrong connection, sponsor loss, transmission accountability | show recovery time, interruption, content continuity |
| local bootstrap authentication | organizational audit, accountability, distribution responsibility | immediate junk-hardware recovery, local onboarding, owner sovereignty |
| hardware failure | warranty, replacement stock, inspected population | isolate the physical unit, tape-level recovery, continue until donations arrive |
| UI warning | operator acknowledgment and audit trace | performer cognitive load, one-handed use, performance continuity |

In Layer A, do not flatten this into one technical score for safe/unsafe or correct/incorrect. Identify the dominant
loss, the risk being transferred, the product's declared purpose, and the affected user.

In Layer B and a README cover, a project may explicitly criticize a philosophy it rejects. Technical respect for an
upstream implementation does not impose neutrality toward a sponsor-first worldview that ranks corporate wallets,
institutional preservation, or enterprise responsibility above a performer's life, expression, and recovery.
ZeroRoomLab does not inherit capital's master merely because capital has more deployments.

Users who require commercial KPI and commercial responsibility avoidance may choose devices designed for those
purposes. A small OSS project is not obligated to reproduce the same priorities cheaply. Open devices matter because
they can implement objectives for users, uses, recovery paths, and expressive sovereignty that a commercial KPI drops.

When a mature upstream component carries a different KPI:

1. Record the behavior, operating conditions, and protected interest as facts.
2. Do not invent the upstream author's intent or malice.
3. Record the target product's original purpose, user KPI, and accepted risk independently.
4. When they conflict, preserve the original purpose and absorb the difference through an adapter, watchdog, policy
   profile, or local implementation.
5. If commercial compatibility is not the trunk objective, return it to a commercial device, external profile, or
   fork. Do not let “being helpful” drain the project into reproducing an existing market.

Mainstream use remains evidence about compatibility, available components, known failures, and maintenance knowledge.
It is evidence for adoption value, not authority to adjudicate the objective.

## 3. Application by engineering field

### 3.1 Safety engineering

Inspect not only Risk, but also the protected future, expected Benefit, original purpose, required resources, safety
margin, and room to exit. Myth is not fuel for accepting danger. It is a reason to demand the safety margin.

### 3.2 Institutional engineering

Ask which people, rights, resources, trust, and re-entry paths a rule, regulation, contract, application, or
credential boundary was created to protect. Do not turn compliance itself into the objective.

### 3.3 Financial engineering

Retain research continuity, gift economy, public goods, optionality, and exploration margin alongside revenue,
cash flow, return, and risk coefficients. Do not collateralize unpaid labor with romance. Connect romance to a
resource design that can sustain it.

### 3.4 Information engineering and KPI design

At every transformation stage, audit whether a proxy has eaten the original purpose. At minimum, record original
purpose, reason for change, removed content, unknowns, impact, and rollback path.

### 3.5 Human factors and GUI design

A GUI is not a thin efficiency skin. It is the cognitive surface where a user distinguishes now, future, danger,
benefit, responsibility, withdrawal, and uncertainty. A mythic presentation must not hide an alarm. An alarm must not
delete future value.

## 4. Cross-audit with infoton engineering

Infoton engineering traces how one input resolves differently under an observer, routing, output, and verifier.
This audit adds the preservation, transformation, and reduction of myth and objective function to that trace.

Minimum recommended record:

```text
ψ: original observation / myth / purpose / request
∇φ: institutional, financial, informational, ergonomic routing
λ: policy, KPI, GUI, contract, code, operational output
Q: evidence, purpose-integrity check, resource-integrity check, unknown, rollback
```

The audit runs in both directions:

1. From the myth side, inspect whether implementation attenuated the objective.
2. From the engineering side, inspect whether myth replaced operating conditions, resources, safety margin, or Evidence.
3. From the infoton side, trace the routing where meaning changed.
4. Attach results to an operation and snapshot, not a personality score.

## 5. Decision matrix

| Purpose/myth | Implementation/resources | Result |
|---|---|---|
| retained | sufficient | candidate mutual mapping; continue verification |
| retained | insufficient | **purpose-exploitation alarm**; myth may not substitute for labor or risk capacity |
| missing | sufficient | candidate KPI, institutional, or GUI drift |
| missing | insufficient | candidate collapse; return purpose and implementation upstream |
| `UNKNOWN` | any | do not infer a quadrant; request confirmation |

## 6. Claim boundary

This contract does not claim that myth or philosophy automatically justifies an engineering decision. It does not
claim that every engineering incident comes from objective-function attenuation.

It claims an operating principle: observe purpose/myth integrity and implementation/resource/verification state
independently, then make the transformation path mutually auditable.
