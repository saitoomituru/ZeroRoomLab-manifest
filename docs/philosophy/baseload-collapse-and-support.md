# Baseload Collapse and the Boundary of Support

## Summary

ZeroRoomLab is not a research lab that sits on top of a stable household, utility, transport, medical, and public-finance base.
It operates where several supporting layers are thinning at the same time: personal credit capacity, the household wallet, shared infrastructure in a small aging settlement, municipal finance, regional medicine and rescue, forestry and flood control, power reception and distribution, and national fiscal choices.

This document calls that condition **multi-layer baseload collapse**.

Because several layers can fail together, support cannot be represented honestly as a simple purchase in which a fixed amount always becomes one named feature, product, or paper.
Resources may go to whichever layer has become the immediate bottleneck: electricity, food, communications, transport, tools, parts, access to medicine or rescue, maintenance of the house and water systems, experimental equipment, or the time required to publish the resulting records.

This is not ambiguity used to hide spending.
Artificially separating survival from research would misdescribe the actual experimental system.

## The layers that are losing their floor

The chain observed by ZeroRoomLab is roughly:

1. personal credit history and liquidity;
2. household operating and replacement costs;
3. shared labor and infrastructure maintenance in a small, aging settlement;
4. the difficulty of sustaining water, wells, flood control, roads, electricity, communications, and forestry together;
5. declining fiscal room at town, district, and prefectural levels;
6. uneven public and insurance burdens for regional medicine, rescue, wildlife damage, disasters, and mobility;
7. national choices concerning monetary sovereignty, deficits, redistribution, and public investment.

These layers are not independent.
Costs, hazards, maintenance work, and accountability that are not absorbed upstream are pushed toward smaller governments, households, and individuals.
Calling the final person “self-reliant” does not solve the problem when money, electricity, water, roads, medicine, and communications are themselves unstable dependencies.

## What ZeroRoomLab can promise

ZeroRoomLab can promise to:

- use received resources against the current bottleneck across research, production, and the minimum operating base;
- publish experiments, failures, repairs, observations, and design decisions where disclosure is possible;
- preserve unfinished branches together with their failure conditions and restart conditions;
- avoid inventing maturity, certainty, or causality to satisfy a sponsor narrative;
- avoid converting support automatically into an unlimited claim on labor or ownership of the resulting work.

## What ZeroRoomLab cannot promise

ZeroRoomLab cannot honestly promise that:

- a fixed amount of support will always become one specified feature or product;
- a research result will be completed by a fixed date;
- returns will scale linearly with the amount received;
- research and survival costs can be fully separated;
- every exploration branch will reach commercialization or deployment.

This is not the absence of planning.
In a multi-layer collapse, the weakest supporting layer must often be repaired first or every research branch above it is lost at once.

## What this repository can return

ZeroRoomLab-manifest is not only a product roadmap.
It is an **advance-scout and first-casualty repository** for last-mile failures that are already appearing around other people: gaps between institutions, unmaintainable equipment, research under severe resource constraints, vendor sunsets, and missing coverage in rural medicine, rescue, wildlife response, and disaster operations.

Its useful outputs include:

- where the assumptions of an existing institution or product broke;
- what could be substituted and what could not;
- the power, communications, tools, skills, and time the workaround required;
- which failures were local and which are likely to recur elsewhere;
- maps that reduce the chance that the next person falls into the same hole.

Support cannot always be tied to one deliverable, but discoveries, failures, repairs, and survivable configurations can still be recorded and converted into reusable public knowledge.

## Yokai ecosystem preservation as the minimum objective

Even before markets or institutions can assign a value, there is a need to observe, praise, and record that a person remained alive, continued exploring, and uncovered something.

ZeroRoomLab calls this minimum objective **yokai ecosystem preservation**.

It does not mean that results are irrelevant.
It means that anomalies, isolated skills, place-specific knowledge, unclassified theories, failure logs, and experiments in which life and research share one system should not disappear merely because existing institutions cannot yet price or classify them.
