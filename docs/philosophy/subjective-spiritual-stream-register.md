# Subjective Spiritual Stream Register

## Purpose

This document is a register for spiritual practitioners, religious practitioners, mediums, shrine practitioners, diviners, artists, researchers, and others to share spiritual or paranormal inputs they experience as subjective truth without reducing those experiences to fiction and without silently promoting them into reproduced natural-science facts.

Its purpose is not to let an authority decide whose experience is correct.
It is to place different accounts side by side: what was received, through which sensory form, in what temporal structure, under which conditions, and through which translation method. From there, participants may look for shared patterns, differences, conversion methods, and observable surfaces.

## Why multiple first-person accounts matter

A single account is easily misclassified as private mythology, a symptom, a performance persona, or a universal objective law.
When multiple practitioners preserve their own accounts side by side, it becomes easier to distinguish:

- person-specific perception styles;
- patterns shared by a tradition, place, ritual, or culture;
- patterns altered by training or tools;
- similar vocabulary describing different experiences;
- different vocabulary describing structurally similar experiences;
- surfaces that may be separable for third-party observation or measurement.

Shared subjectivity is not a substitute for objectivity.
It is both a map of the ore body before objective study and a cultural record that prevents non-objectifiable experience from disappearing.

## Suggested fields

Participants may record, where appropriate:

1. **Reception form**
   - image, voice, words, bodily sensation, pressure, smell, temperature, dream, intuition, movement sense, spatial structure, synchronicity, other
2. **Temporal form**
   - instant image, continuous stream, repetition, premonition, ritual-only, dream continuing after waking, other
3. **Resolution and structure**
   - photorealistic, symbolic, abstract, multi-viewpoint, ultra-wide-angle, layered, narrative, mathematical, geometric, technical-system form, other
4. **Perceived source**
   - own thought, another person, deity, spirit, ancestor, place, extraterrestrial or cosmic being, unknown source, no perceived source
5. **Conditions**
   - location, time, ritual, prayer, sleep, creative work, crisis, specific tools, group practice, natural environment, unknown
6. **Translation method**
   - image, song, dance, prose, divination, diagram, code, ritual, consultation, body technique, not recordable
7. **Shareability**
   - self-only, partially shared with witnesses, later cross-check possible, third-party observable, instrument candidate, reproducibility unknown
8. **Practitioner's own claim layer**
   - faith statement, subjective truth, hypothesis, artistic expression, natural-science claim, unclassified

## Example entry: fusamofu / Mitsuru Saitō

### Multidimensional spiritual-image reception

The practitioner reports receiving deities, spiritual beings, mythic structures, and relations among concepts as dense visual and spatial images rather than words alone.

The reported tendencies include:

- near-photoreal texture;
- ultra-wide-angle scenes containing multiple viewpoints and time axes;
- relational structures unfolding in more than a flat diagram;
- deities and concepts felt as if they were constraint-bearing conceptual spiritual armatures;
- perception as a continuing stream of state and meaning rather than a single still image.

The practitioner can project part of this experience into images, prose, stories, structural theory, and diagrams.
Because the source experience is high-density, sharing requires compression and lower-dimensional projection.

### Deity as stream rather than fixed code

Within the practitioner's faith, truth is not experienced as one frozen sentence or fixed code artifact. It moves as a stream through situation, relation, observer, and time.

A deity is likewise not confined to a static configuration or single personality. In the practitioner's software metaphor, a deity is closer to a runner executing a stream arriving from outside the universe.

This is not a claim that deity is reducible to software.
Software structure is being used to project one surface of the experienced relation into a lower-dimensional explanation.

### Structural correspondence between myth and technology

The practitioner reports perceiving structural correspondences among myth, social systems, AI, software, networks, disasters, and religious narratives.

Examples include the Tower of Babel, flood myths, NPM or Webpack dependency collapse, and large-system failure caused by configuration files. These may be felt not as unrelated stories but as similar structures projected into different layers.

Such correspondence does not by itself establish identical natural-science causation.
For research, it may be mined into comparable structures, recurrence conditions, failure patterns, and design lessons.

## Boundary for comparison and research

Shared patterns in this register do not by themselves demonstrate paranormal causation under natural science.
At the same time, lack of such demonstration is not a license to erase the subjective truth or religious meaning held by multiple practitioners.

Research should separate:

- first-person experience records;
- comparison across accounts;
- comparison of ritual, environmental, and tool conditions;
- third-party observation;
- instrument measurement;
- reproduction tests;
- religious and theological interpretation.

ZeroRoomLab treats this separation not as a denial of faith but as range control for expanding what can be shared without destroying the source experience.

## Contribution and editing principles

- Preserve the practitioner's own vocabulary where possible.
- Do not silently translate an account into the editor's tradition or worldview.
- Do not automatically classify an account as medicine, psychology, religion, fraud, or art.
- Do not use the register to justify dangerous or unlawful conduct.
- Do not publish private information, secret rites, or third-party experiences without consent.
- Preserve disagreement as a separate annotation layer rather than deleting the original account.
