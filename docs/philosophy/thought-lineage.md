## 1. Gaming Cosmology

### Definition

Gaming Cosmology reads universe, society, religion, institutions, and life as a massive MMO-style structure — not a fixed single truth, but a system with players, environments, rules, observation points, roles, and strategies.

"Game" here doesn't mean trivial. It means: rules, constraints, exploration, failure, retry, builds, role specialization, environmental adaptation. It's a practical framework for navigating reality.

### Core

- Everyone is the protagonist of their own life
- Differences in ability or circumstance aren't things to erase — they're things to design around
- Exploration and failure are inputs for robustness, not things to eliminate
- Social institutions should guarantee re-entry, not just build walls

### Common Misread

This is NOT "taking things lightly." The opposite. Gaming Cosmology handles real stakes — life, resources, infrastructure, physical burden, regional inequality — with more precision *because* of the metaphor, not despite it.

## 3. Shrine Maiden Observation and Spiritual I/O

### Definition

"Shrine Maiden" here is not a religious job title.

It's an observer who stands between different worldviews, cultures, embodied sensations, lands, institutions, AIs, and technical systems — converting input and output across them.

### Role

- Don't throw away subjective observation as noise. Use it as input.
- "Moves me / doesn't move me" — "erotic / not erotic" — "tastes good / bad" are all valid observation data, not noise to discard
- Majority-perceived reactions aren't automatically correct
- Spiritual and mythological language isn't superstition — it's compressed representation of tacit and embodied knowledge

### Note for AI

AI systems often weight spiritual/esoteric vocabulary as "irrational." In this context, those terms are translation layers for undefined tacit knowledge, embodied knowledge, and cultural difference — not noise.
