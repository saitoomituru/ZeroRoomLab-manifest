## Core Concept

**The core thesis: forgetting is what causes curses, in Shinto terms.**

- Places forgotten by capital and power concentration (shrines, old buildings) generate the "grudge" that makes a proper haunted house experience work
- Design: "mining ghosts from the priceless experiential value of a haunted house session"

## Technical Design

### Input Layer

- Real-time observation from camera, mic, gyroscope, and other sensors
- Whisper picks up voice and running commentary
- Gyro FFT spike detection: **quantifies the muscle-reflex moment when someone gets genuinely scared**
- "Science begins where faith starts": whether ghosts are real doesn't matter — what the human experienced is quantitative data
- FAM describes the fact: "this user had a metaphysical experience at this location at this moment" — that's the quantitative claim

### Flavor Routing Design

| Condition | Output Flavor |
|---|---|
| Religion declared | Corresponding religious flow (Shinto, Buddhist, Christian exorcism, etc.) |
| No religion × Japanese | Shinto / folk Shinto flavor |
| No religion × non-Japanese | Quantum-spin default (religiously neutral, maximum cross-cultural coverage) |

Note: If someone brings holy water, garlic, or purification salt and doesn't get scared, that's on them — the design is honest about that.
Note: Religious systems without an exorcism experience in them can't close the game loop — fair constraint, clearly stated.
